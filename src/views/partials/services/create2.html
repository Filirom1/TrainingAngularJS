Reminder: services and modules

  • As we told before...
  • Any controller/service/directive/filter is associated to a module
  • Modules can depend on other modules, which gives them access to services/.../filters provided by the others modules
  • Knowing that, you can split your application module between submodules
//Define a custom module for your services angular.module('myModule.services', ['ngRoute', 'ngResource']) .service('s1', function(){....}) .service('s1', function(){....}) ... //Do the same for controllers/directives/filters angular.module('myModule.controllers', []).controller('c1', function(){....}) ... angular.module('myModule.directives', []).directive('d1', function(){....}) ... angular.module('myModule.filters', []).filter('f1', function(){....}) ... //Finally, declare your application module angular.module('myModule', ['myModule.controllers', 'myModule.services', 'myModule.directives', 'myModule.filters'] );
